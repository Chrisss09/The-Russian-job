Moscow



Top 5 must see in Moscow!


1. St. Basil's Cathedral

On the order of Ivan the Terrible, his loyal men built the cathedral to celebrate the capture of Kazan from the Mongol forces in 1552. The cathedral was built in 1560 and Ivan was so besotted with the building that it was said that he gouged out the eyes of the slaves that built it so that they couldn't build anything else that was this beautiful. The cathedral is now a museum where you can admire the artwork outside and within. The beautiful paintings and architecture will leave you mesmerised but be careful of the narrow staircases and low ceilings.


2. Lenin's Mausoleum

In 1917 the Russian Revolusionaries took the world by storm under the command of Vladamir Lenin by dethroning the Romanoff Monarchy after their 300 year reign. After his tragic death in 1924, the whole of Russia mourned and to pay their respects they built a Mausoleum and preserved Lenin's body and memory. Now you can visit this by walking along the Kremlin wall and go inside the Mausoleum to pay your respects and witness the icon: Vladamir Lenin.

3. State Historical Museum

Opened in 1894, the museum was the result of a 20-year project to consolidate multiple archaeological and anthropological collections into a single museum. This is a must for anyone that wants to learn all about Russian history as it covers 1,000 years of Russian history and the history of Northeastern Eurasian civilizations.


4. Fallen Monument Park

This is a park known for being where statues were disposed of during the fall of the USSR. The park contains statues from the golden literature error, the Soviet error, the Cold War and many more. This is a nice little park that runs along the river Moscow, on a nice day go and sit down with a drink and something to eat and admire the scenery.


5. The Kremlin

The Kremlin is one of Russia's top tourist attractions. It is the building that has held the likes of Ivan the Great, Ivan the Terrible, Lenin, Stalin, Gorbachev, Yeltsin and now Putin. To see the whole of the Kremlin you will at least need a full day due to the many cathedrals that the kremlin holds;

Great food in Moscow


Moscow has some of the best food anywhere if you know where to look, but people are often puzzled when I tell them how much I miss food I used to eat there, during the two years I worked in Moscow. This is not to say that all of it was Russian food–that's like saying you miss NYC for its bagels and NYC style pizza. Certainly there are traditional Russian comfort foods I miss, but Moscow, similar to NYC, offers an array of international restaurants featuring everything from sushi (which many Muscovites have adored for years) to fine French cuisine to traditional dishes from Georgia, Uzbekistan, and the other former Soviet republics. I even miss things I used to be able to get in my local (albeit high end) grocery store, which Whole Foods and other stores in NYC don't carry.

So forget long bread lines and any misconceptions you may have about Russian food or Moscow–if you enjoy great food, there's plenty to keep you happy in Moscow.


Some of the best food to try in Moscow is the following;

Pelmeni

The wild mushroom pelmeni, is among the best in town. Try it for a more relaxed lunch if you're not up to a very expensive formal dinner.

Khachapuri

Georgian food was always beloved in the former Soviet Union, given all the fresh fruits and vegetables grown there, Georgian wine, and especially these piping hot breads stuffed with Georgian Sulguni cheese. Cafe Khachapuri is a great place to try them and has several Moscow locations, with English menus if you don't read Russian.

Black Currant Pastry

These are only made at Volkonsky bakeries in Russia and possibly Kiev. The tartness of the plump and juicy black currants contrast perfectly with the sweet and buttery croissant pastry.

Syrniki with Homemade Jam

Syrniki are Russian farmers cheese pancakes– something like a denser version of ricotta pancakes–and they are heavenly when spread with some homemade jam, as with these versions from the copious breakfast buffet at Les Menus at the Lotte Hotel Moscow.

Borscht

Borscht is one of the dishes Russia is most known for, and when done well, it really is the perfect hearty winter soup, filled with nourishing julienned beets and carrots, chunks of potato and beef, all in a gorgeously magenta hued broth and finished with a sprinkling of cilantro and a generous dollop of smetana, Russian sour cream. I'm partial to the version at Mari Vanna, a high end chain with restaurants in Moscow, St. Petersburg, London–there's even a Mari Vanna in New York. But even many of the versions served as part of the “business lunch” specials around town are quite good.
